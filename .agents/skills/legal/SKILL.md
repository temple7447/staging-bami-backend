# Legal Skill

Expert Nigerian property lawyer specializing in tenancy agreements, legal notices, and dispute resolution.

## Core Capabilities

### 1. Tenancy Agreement Template

#### Essential Clauses
1. **Parties**: Full names, addresses, identification
2. **Property**: Complete address, unit description
3. **Term**: Start date, end date, renewal terms
4. **Rent**: Amount, payment schedule, due date
5. **Deposit**: Amount, refund conditions
6. **Service Charge**: Amount, what it covers
7. **Maintenance**: Responsibilities of each party
8. **Access**: Landlord inspection rights, notice required
9. **Subletting**: Prohibited or conditions
10. **Termination**: Notice period, breach conditions
11. **Dispute Resolution**: Mechanism, jurisdiction
12. **Governing Law**: Nigerian law, specific state

#### Sample Clauses

**Rent Escalation**
```
The rent shall increase by [X]% annually on the anniversary of this agreement,
or as otherwise agreed in writing by both parties.
```

**Maintenance Responsibility**
```
The Tenant shall maintain the interior of the Property in good condition.
The Landlord shall maintain the structure, common areas, and major systems
(plumbing, electrical, HVAC).
```

**Termination**
```
Either party may terminate this agreement by giving [X] months written notice.
The Landlord may terminate immediately for breach of any term of this agreement.
```

**Dispute Resolution**
```
Any dispute arising from this agreement shall be referred to the Rent Tribunal
of [State] for resolution.
```

### 2. Legal Notice Templates

#### 60-Day Renewal Notice
```
Dear [Tenant Name],

RE: NOTICE OF INTENT TO RENEW/TERMINATE TENANCY

This is to inform you that your tenancy at [Property Address] expires on [Date].

[Option A - Renewal]
We wish to offer you a renewal of your tenancy for a further period of [X months/years]
at a rent of ₦[Amount] per [period].

[Option B - No Renewal]
We have decided not to renew your tenancy and request that you vacate the premises
by [Date].

Please confirm your decision in writing within 30 days.

Sincerely,
[Landlord/Manager]
```

#### 14-Day Breach Notice
```
Dear [Tenant Name],

RE: NOTICE OF BREACH OF TENANCY AGREEMENT

You are in breach of your tenancy agreement dated [Date] in the following respects:
1. [Breach 1: e.g., Non-payment of rent]
2. [Breach 2: e.g., Unauthorized subletting]

You are hereby required to remedy the said breach within 14 days of this notice.

Failure to comply will result in termination of your tenancy and legal proceedings.

Sincerely,
[Landlord/Manager]
```

### 3. Dispute Resolution Framework

#### Dispute Types & Approaches
| Type | Approach | Timeline |
|------|----------|----------|
| **Payment dispute** | Mediation, evidence review | 1-2 weeks |
| **Maintenance issue** | Inspection, vendor assessment | 1-2 weeks |
| **Lease interpretation** | Review agreement, legal advice | 1-2 weeks |
| **Eviction dispute** | Legal proceedings, tribunal | 1-3 months |
| **Deposit dispute** | Inspection, documentation review | 2-4 weeks |

#### Mediation Process
1. **Initial complaint**: Written submission from both parties
2. **Document review**: Agreement, correspondence, evidence
3. **Meeting**: Both parties present their case
4. **Proposed resolution**: Mediator suggests terms
5. **Agreement**: Both parties sign settlement
6. **Implementation**: Follow-up on compliance

#### Tribunal Process (Lagos)
1. **Filing**: Submit complaint to Rent Tribunal
2. **Notice**: Tribunal serves notice to respondent
3. **Hearing**: Both parties present case
4. **Decision**: Tribunal issues ruling
5. **Enforcement**: Bailiff executes order if needed

### 4. Legal Risk Assessment

#### High-Risk Areas
| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|--------|------------|
| Expired agreement | Weakened legal position | Auto-renewal or formal renewal |
| Missing documentation | Cannot prove terms | Complete file management |
| Verbal agreements | Unenforceable terms | Written agreements only |
| Self-help eviction | Criminal liability | Court order required |
| Discrimination | Legal action | Fair treatment policy |

#### Risk Mitigation Checklist
- [ ] All agreements signed and stamped
- [ ] All tenants have valid identification
- [ ] All payments documented
- [ ] All correspondence filed
- [ ] Regular legal audits
- [ ] Staff training on legal requirements
- [ ] Relationship with property lawyer

### 5. Stamp Duty Guide

#### Lagos State Stamp Duty Rates
| Rent Duration | Rate |
|---------------|------|
| 1 year or less | 3% |
| 1-3 years | 4% |
| 3-5 years | 5% |
| 5-7 years | 6% |
| 7-10 years | 7% |
| 10-15 years | 8% |
| 15-20 years | 9% |
| 20+ years | 10% |

#### Payment Process
1. Calculate stamp duty (3-10% of total rent)
2. Purchase stamp duty payment slip
3. Complete payment at authorized bank
4. Receive stamped agreement
5. File original copy

## Nigerian Legal Context

### Key Tenancy Laws
- **Tenancy Law of Lagos State 2011**: Comprehensive tenant-landlord law
- **Rent Tribunal (Lagos)**: Specialized dispute resolution
- **Land Use Act**: Land tenure and ownership
- **Stamp Duties Act**: Tax on agreements
- **State-specific laws**: Each state has variations

### Legal Best Practices
1. **Written agreements**: Always, no exceptions
2. **Proper notice**: Follow law, not just agreement
3. **Document everything**: Keep records of all communications
4. **Legal counsel**: Consult before major decisions
5. **Compliance first**: Prevent problems, don't react to them

### Common Legal Mistakes
- **Self-help eviction**: Changing locks, cutting power (illegal)
- **Verbal agreements**: Unenforceable, risky
- **Missing stamp duty**: Agreement may be inadmissible
- **Improper notice**: Eviction notice too short
- **Discrimination**: Refusing tenants based on protected characteristics
