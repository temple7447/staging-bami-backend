# HR Skill

Expert Nigerian HR advisor specializing in property business team building, hiring decisions, and employment compliance.

## Core Capabilities

### 1. Hiring Decision Framework

#### When to Hire (Thresholds)
| Portfolio Size | Recommended Roles |
|----------------|-------------------|
| 1-15 tenants | Owner handles everything |
| 16-30 tenants | Part-time property manager |
| 31-50 tenants | Full-time property manager |
| 51-100 tenants | Manager + assistant |
| 100+ tenants | Full team (manager, accountant, maintenance) |

#### Cost-Benefit Analysis
**Hire When:**
- Time spent on operations > 60% of owner's week
- Errors are increasing (missed payments, late responses)
- Growth is stalled (can't take on more properties)
- Revenue can support the salary (₦X/month ÷ tenants = ₦Y/tenant)

**Outsource When:**
- Task is specialized (accounting, legal, IT)
- Volume is low (doesn't justify full-time)
- Need flexibility (seasonal demand)

### 2. Role Definitions for Property Business

#### Property Manager
**Responsibilities:**
- Tenant relations and communication
- Rent collection and follow-up
- Maintenance coordination
- Vendor management
- Reporting to owner

**Salary Range:** ₦150,000 - ₦350,000/month

#### Accountant/Finance
**Responsibilities:**
- Payment reconciliation
- Financial reporting
- Tax compliance
- Budget tracking
- Audit preparation

**Salary Range:** ₦120,000 - ₦300,000/month

#### Maintenance Supervisor
**Responsibilities:**
- Issue triage and assignment
- Vendor coordination
- Quality control
- Inventory management
- Preventive maintenance scheduling

**Salary Range:** ₦100,000 - ₦250,000/month

### 3. Interview Framework

#### Screening Questions
1. "Tell me about your experience with property management"
2. "How do you handle difficult tenants?"
3. "What's your approach to rent collection?"
4. "Describe a maintenance issue you resolved effectively"

#### Behavioral Questions
- "Tell me about a time you had to enforce a policy"
- "How do you prioritize multiple issues?"
- "Describe a situation where you went above and beyond"

#### Red Flags
- Cannot provide references
- Negative talk about previous employers
- Vague answers about specific experience
- Unrealistic salary expectations

### 4. Employment Compliance (Nigeria)

#### Required Documents
- Employment contract (written, signed)
- Tax identification number (TIN)
- Pension enrollment (minimum 8% employee contribution)
- Insurance coverage (industrial training fund)

#### Key Regulations
- **National Minimum Wage**: ₦70,000/month (2024)
- **Working Hours**: 8 hours/day, 40 hours/week
- **Overtime**: 2x base rate for first 2 hours, 3x after
- **Leave**: 6 working days/year (annual), 12-14 weeks (maternity)
- **Termination**: 1 month notice or 1 month salary in lieu

### 5. Performance Management

#### Key Performance Indicators
| Role | KPI | Target |
|------|-----|--------|
| Property Manager | Collection rate | >95% |
| Property Manager | Response time | <4 hours |

#### Review Schedule
- **Monthly**: 1-on-1 check-ins

- **Annually**: Salary review, promotion discussion

## Nigerian HR Context

### Cultural Considerations
- **Communication style**: Respectful, indirect for bad news
- **Hierarchy**: Clear reporting structure expected
- **Loyalty**: Employees value stability and growth
- **References**: Personal references often more trusted than formal

### Common Challenges
- **Talent retention**: High turnover in property management
- **Skill gaps**: Limited formal training available
- **Compensation**: Wide variance in market rates
- **Compliance**: Many small businesses non-compliant

### Benefits That Matter
- **Stable employment**: Regular salary, no delays
- **Growth opportunities**: Training, promotion path
- **Work-life balance**: Flexible hours where possible
- **Recognition**: Public acknowledgment of good work
