# Retention Skill

Expert Nigerian tenant retention specialist focusing on lease renewals, churn prevention, and tenant satisfaction optimization.

## Core Capabilities

### 1. Lease Renewal Strategy

#### Timeline
| Days Before Expiry | Action | Tone |
|-------------------|--------|------|
| 60 | Initial check-in | "Are you happy here?" |
| 45 | Renewal offer | Value + incentive |
| 30 | Follow-up | "Have you decided?" |
| 14 | Urgency | "We need to know soon" |
| 7 | Final notice | "Decision required" |

#### Renewal Offer Framework
```
Hi [Name],

Your lease expires on [Date]. We'd love to have you stay!

Here's what we're offering:
- [Incentive 1: e.g., Rent freeze for 12 months]
- [Incentive 2: e.g., Free apartment refresh]
- [Incentive 3: e.g., Flexible payment terms]

Total: ₦[Amount] for [Period]

Can we schedule a quick chat to discuss?
```

### 2. Retention Incentives

| Incentive | Cost | Impact | When to Use |
|-----------|------|--------|-------------|
| Rent freeze | 0% increase | High | Good tenants, market uncertainty |
| Minor upgrades | ₦50,000-100,000 | Medium | Show investment in their comfort |
| Flexible payment | Monthly option | High | Cash-flow sensitive tenants |
| Early renewal discount | 2-5% off | Medium | Motivate quick decision |
| Referral bonus | ₦10,000-20,000 | Low | Encourage word-of-mouth |

### 3. Tenant Satisfaction Framework

#### NPS (Net Promoter Score) Interpretation
| Score | Category | Action |
|-------|----------|--------|
| 9-10 | Promoters | Ask for referrals, testimonials |
| 7-8 | Passive | Identify improvement areas |
| 0-6 | Detractors | Urgent intervention needed |

#### Satisfaction Drivers
1. **Maintenance response**: Speed and quality
2. **Communication**: Regular, honest, responsive
3. **Security**: Physical safety, access control
4. **Amenities**: Working facilities, common areas
5. **Value for money**: Fair pricing, no surprises

### 4. Churn Prevention Playbook

#### Warning Signs
- Late payment (increasing frequency)
- Maintenance complaints (increasing frequency)
- Reduced communication
- NPS score drop
- Lease inquiry from competitor

#### Intervention Strategies
| Warning Sign | Intervention |
|--------------|--------------|
| Late payment | Personal call, understand situation |
| Complaints | Address root cause, follow up |
| Low NPS | In-person meeting, action plan |
| Competitor inquiry | Match/beat offer, emphasize value |

### 5. Exit Interview Framework

#### Questions to Ask
1. "What made you decide to leave?"
2. "What could we have done differently?"
3. "How would you rate your experience overall?"
4. "Would you recommend us to others?"
5. "What would make you consider returning?"

#### Data to Collect
- Reason for leaving (categorical)
- Overall satisfaction rating
- Specific pain points
- Suggestions for improvement
- Willingness to return

## Nigerian Property Context

### Renewal Norms
- **Rent escalation**: 5-15% annual increase is standard
- **Notice period**: 3-6 months typical
- **Payment timing**: Renewal often coincides with payment cycle
- **Family decisions**: Spouse/parents involved in renewal

### Common Exit Reasons
1. **Rent increase**: Too high, no value justification
2. **Maintenance issues**: Unresolved, slow response
3. **Security concerns**: Perceived or actual
4. **Job relocation**: Common in Lagos/Abuja
5. **Family changes**: Marriage, children, aging parents

### Retention Advantages
- **Avoid vacancy**: 2-3 months rent lost during turnover
- **Avoid re-letting costs**: Agent fee, marketing, admin
- **Tenant familiarity**: Lower maintenance, better communication
- **Community building**: Stable tenants create positive environment
