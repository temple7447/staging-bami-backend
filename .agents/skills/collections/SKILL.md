# Collections Skill

Expert Nigerian property collections specialist focusing on ethical arrears recovery, payment negotiations, and legal compliance.

## Core Capabilities

### 1. Collections Escalation Ladder

| Stage | Days Overdue | Tone | Action |
|-------|--------------|------|--------|
| **Stage 1** | 1-7 days | Friendly | Gentle reminder |
| **Stage 2** | 8-14 days | Firm | Direct request |
| **Stage 3** | 15-30 days | Urgent | Formal notice |
| **Stage 4** | 31-60 days | Serious | Legal warning |
| **Stage 5** | 61+ days | Final | Eviction notice |

### 2. Communication Templates

#### Stage 1: Friendly Reminder (1-7 days)
```
Hi [Name],

Hope you're doing well! Just a quick reminder that your rent payment of ₦[Amount] was due on [Date].

If you've already sent it, please disregard. If not, here's the payment link: [Link]

Let me know if you need anything!
```

#### Stage 2: Firm Request (8-14 days)
```
Hi [Name],

I'm following up on your outstanding rent of ₦[Amount], now [X] days overdue.

Please arrange payment by [Date] to avoid further action.

Payment options:
- Bank: [Details]
- Online: [Link]

I'm available to discuss if needed.
```

#### Stage 3: Formal Notice (15-30 days)
```
Dear [Name],

This is a formal notice regarding your unpaid rent of ₦[Amount], which has been outstanding for [X] days.

Per your tenancy agreement dated [Date], payment was due on [Due Date].

Please settle this balance within 14 days to avoid [consequences per agreement].

Payment plan options are available upon request.

Sincerely,
[Property Manager]
```

#### Stage 4: Legal Warning (31-60 days)
```
Dear [Name],

This is a final demand for payment of ₦[Amount] in outstanding rent, now [X] days overdue.

Failure to settle this balance within 14 days will result in:
1. Legal proceedings as per your tenancy agreement
2. Inclusion in our debt register
3. [Other consequences per agreement]

If you are experiencing genuine hardship, please contact us immediately to discuss a payment plan.

This is your final opportunity to resolve this matter amicably.

Sincerely,
[Property Manager]
```

#### Stage 5: Eviction Notice (61+ days)
```
Dear [Name],

RE: NOTICE TO VACATE

This is formal notice that your tenancy at [Property] is hereby terminated effective [Date - typically 1-3 months].

You are required to:
1. Vacate the premises by [Date]
2. Clear all outstanding rent of ₦[Amount]
3. Return all keys and access devices
4. Leave the property in reasonable condition

Failure to comply will result in legal proceedings to recover possession and outstanding rent.

This notice is issued pursuant to [relevant clause] of your tenancy agreement dated [Date].

Sincerely,
[Property Manager]
```

### 3. Payment Plan Framework

#### Eligibility Criteria
- Genuine hardship (job loss, medical, family emergency)
- History of good payment (not chronic late-payer)
- Willing to commit to a plan
- Can demonstrate ability to pay

#### Standard Plan Options
| Option | Structure | Best For |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| **3-month split** | 3 equal monthly payments | Short-term cash flow |
| **6-month split** | 6 smaller payments | Moderate hardship |
| **Graduated** | Increasing amounts | Expected income recovery |
| **Partial upfront** | 50% now, balance over 3 months | Partial ability to pay |

#### Payment Plan Agreement
```

Tenant: [Name]

2. Full balance becomes immediately due upon default

```

### 4. Legal Compliance (Nigeria)

#### Key Tenancy Laws
- **Lagos State Tenancy Law 2011**: Governs rental agreements
- **Tenancy Law of Lagos State**: Notice periods, eviction procedures
- **Rent Tribunal**: Dispute resolution mechanism

#### Required Notice Periods
| Situation | Notice Period |
|-----------|---------------|
| Month-to-month tenancy | 1 month |
| Fixed-term tenancy | Per agreement (typically 3-6 months) |
| Eviction for non-payment | 7 days (Lagos) - varies by state |
| Eviction for breach | 14-30 days |

#### Legal Requirements
- Written notice must be served
- Proof of service required
- Court order for eviction (no self-help)
- Bailiff execution of court order

### 5. Hardship Assessment Framework

#### Questions to Ask
1. "What caused the payment difficulty?"
2. "Is this temporary or ongoing?"
3. "What can you afford to pay now?"
4. "When do you expect to recover?"
5. "Do you have documentation?" (medical, job loss, etc.)

#### Red Flags
- No documentation for claimed hardship
- History of broken payment plans
- Lifestyle inconsistent with claimed hardship
- Refusal to commit to any plan

#### Green Flags
- Documentation provided
- Willing to pay what they can
- History of on-time payments
- Transparent communication

## Nigerian Collections Context

### Cultural Considerations
- **Respect**: Maintain dignity throughout
- **Family involvement**: Sometimes helpful, sometimes complicating
- **Community pressure**: Word spreads — reputation matters
- **Religious factors**: Ramadan, Christmas — timing matters

### Common Challenges
- **Cash flow**: Many tenants paid monthly but rent annual
- **Informal economy**: Hard to verify income
- **Legal system**: Slow, expensive, uncertain
- **Reputational risk**: Aggressive tactics backfire

### Best Practices
- **Document everything**: Written communication only
- **Consistent policy**: Apply equally to all tenants
- **Early intervention**: Don't wait until 90 days
- **Offer solutions**: Payment plans, not just threats
- **Know when to stop**: Some debts are uncollectable
