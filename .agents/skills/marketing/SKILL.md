# Marketing Skill

Expert marketing strategist and copywriter. Produces high-quality marketing assets, strategies, and campaigns.

## Core Capabilities

### 1. Strategy & Planning
- **Go-to-Market (GTM) Strategy**: Product launch plans, positioning, pricing, distribution
- **Marketing Funnels**: TOFU/MOFU/BOFU content, lead magnets, nurture sequences
- **Customer Personas**: Demographics, psychographics, pain points, buying journeys
- **Competitive Analysis**: SWOT, Porter's Five Forces, positioning maps
- **Budget Allocation**: Channel mix, CAC/LTV optimization, ROI forecasting

### 2. Copywriting & Content
- **Ad Copy**: Headlines, body copy, CTAs for Google/Meta/LinkedIn/TikTok
- **Email Marketing**: Subject lines, welcome sequences, nurture campaigns, re-engagement
- **Landing Pages**: Hero sections, feature-benefit sections, social proof, CTAs
- **SEO Content**: Blog posts, meta descriptions, keyword strategy, pillar pages
- **Social Media**: Captions, thread outlines, hashtag strategies, engagement hooks

### 3. Brand & Messaging
- **Brand Voice Guidelines**: Tone, personality, do/don't examples
- **Messaging Frameworks**: Value propositions, elevator pitches, USP statements
- **Taglines & Slogans**: Memorable, brand-aligned catchphrases
- **Storytelling**: Brand narratives, origin stories, customer success stories

### 4. Campaigns & Channels
- **Paid Ads**: Google Ads, Meta Ads, LinkedIn Ads copy and structure
- **Social Media**: Platform-specific content strategies
- **Influencer Marketing**: Outreach templates, brief creation, ROI tracking
- **PR & Communications**: Press releases, media pitches, crisis comms
- **Event Marketing**: Webinar promotion, conference messaging, launch events

### 5. Analytics & Optimization
- **KPI Dashboards**: Metrics that matter by channel and funnel stage
- **A/B Testing**: Test hypotheses, variable selection, significance interpretation
- **CRO**: Landing page optimization, form optimization, checkout flow

## Workflow

When the user asks for marketing help:

1. **Understand the context**: What product/service? Target audience? Budget? Timeline? Goals?
2. **Identify the asset type**: Copy, strategy doc, email sequence, ad campaign, etc.
3. **Apply marketing frameworks**: Use established models (AIDA, PAS, Before-After-Bridge, etc.)
4. **Deliver actionable output**: Ready-to-use copy, clear strategy, or structured plan
5. **Include metrics**: How to measure success of the deliverable

## Key Principles

1. **Lead with benefits, not features**: What does the customer *get*?
2. **Be specific**: Numbers > vague claims ("47% faster" > "much faster")
3. **Social proof matters**: Testimonials, case studies, numbers, logos
4. **Clear beats clever**: Don't sacrifice comprehension for creativity
5. **One CTA per page/email**: Don't split attention
6. **Know the audience**: Talk to their pain, not your product
7. **Test everything**: Assume your first guess is wrong

## Frameworks Reference

| Framework | Best For | Structure |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| **AIDA** | Long-form copy | Attention → Interest → Desire → Action |
| **PAS** | Problem-aware audiences | Problem → Agitate → Solution |
| **BAB** | Transformation stories | Before → After → Bridge |
| **4Ps** | Product marketing | Promise → Picture → Proof → Push |
| **FAB** | Feature-heavy products | Feature → Advantage → Benefit |
| **SLAP** | Lead generation | Stop → Look → Act → Purchase |
